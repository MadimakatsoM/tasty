About Our Community Mission

Rescuing surplus food and serving free, nutritious meals.

The Community Kitchen

Tasty isn't a traditional restaurant; we are a **food rescue initiative** and **community hub**. We operate on a simple principle: millions go hungry while tons of perfectly good food are wasted daily.

We partner with local grocery stores, farms, and food businesses to collect their surplus ingredients. Our dedicated chefs and volunteers transform this rescued food into delicious, high-quality meals, served free of charge to anyone in the community.

This is where the 'needs your food' idea comes to life: **We need donations of surplus food** (from businesses) and **volunteer time** (from individuals) to continue our mission of reducing waste and fighting hunger.

How to Donate Food

  • Our Journey to Zero Waste

  • The Initial Spark

    Our founders, a chef and an environmental activist, met at a local food bank and realized the scale of the food waste problem. The idea for Tasty was born: an upscale solution to a down-to-earth problem.

  • Securing the Kitchen Space

    Through community crowdfunding and a generous donation, we secured our first commercial kitchen space. This allowed us to process larger food donations safely and efficiently.

  • First Corporate Partnership

    We established our first formal food donation agreement with 'Fresh Grocer' supermarket, committing to regular pickups of surplus produce, baked goods, and dairy nearing their best-by date.


  • Expanding Our Reach

  • Launch of Volunteer Program

    To keep up with demand, we launched a dedicated volunteer program, engaging local residents to help with food prep, serving, and logistics. Our team grew to over 50 regular volunteers.

  • Opening the Second Hub

    Based on the success of our initial location, we expanded by opening a second hub in a neighboring district, allowing us to serve two distinct low-income communities.

  • Zero-Waste Cooking Classes

    We introduced public cooking classes focused on preserving and utilizing food scraps, helping local families reduce their own household food waste and improve budgeting.
